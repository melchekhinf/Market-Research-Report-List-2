<p><p>The multifocal diffractive lens market includes two main types: diffractive bifocal lenses and diffractive trifocal lenses. Diffractive bifocal lenses are designed to provide clear vision for both near and distance objects, with a distinct zone for each. On the other hand, diffractive trifocal lenses offer three distinct zones for near, intermediate, and distance vision, allowing wearers to see clearly at all three distances. Both types of lenses utilize diffractive optics to split light and focus it onto different areas of the retina, providing improved vision for individuals with presbyopia.</p></p>
