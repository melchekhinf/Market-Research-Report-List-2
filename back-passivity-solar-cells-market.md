<p><p>Back passivation solar cells are a type of solar cell technology that aims to enhance the efficiency and performance of photovoltaic systems. There are three main types of back passivation solar cells: IBC (Interdigitated Back-Contact) solar cells, EWT (Emitter Wrap-Through) solar cells, and MWT (Metal Wrap-Through) solar cells. IBC cells utilize contacts on the backside to improve efficiency, while EWT cells route the front-side emitter contacts through the rear side, reducing shading. MWT cells also route the electrical connections through the rear side but use a metal wrap-through design for improved conductivity. Each of these technologies offers unique advantages in terms of efficiency and overall solar cell performance.</p></p>
