<p><p>Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC) is a refining process used to convert heavy crude oil into lighter products. There are two types of FCC market configurations: side-by-side and stacked. In side-by-side FCC, two or more FCC units are placed next to each other, allowing simultaneous operation and independent product specifications. Stacked FCC, on the other hand, involves multiple FCC units stacked vertically, resulting in increased processing capacity. Both configurations offer flexibility, higher efficiency, and increased production rates, catering to different refinery requirements and market demands.</p></p>
